Research Areas

Addressing clinical needs through intelligent technological solutions. Our work spans robotics, simulation, and sensor integration to improve cardiovascular health outcomes.

Interactive Wearables

ACTIVE-CLC: Active cardiac locomotor coupling

The ACTIVE project investigates the relationship and the interaction between cardiovascular and locomotor system during normal daily activities and in laboratory. Starting from this knowledge, we aim to explore the beneficial effects of home-based cardiac rehabilitation treatment for patients with heart failure using a wearable sensor system with an auditory signal for the synchronization of the heart and movement.

Contact: Aurora Rosato
Funded by: Promobilia Stiftelsen and Åke Wiberg Foundation

Experimental

Test-rigs for patient-specific insight

In recent years, biomimetic design from the field of soft robotics play an increasing role in mimicking physiological phenomena on the benchtop. From an ethical perspective, benchtop and computational testing mitigates the need for excessive animal testing, a common practice in medical device innovations. Our research focuses on enabling patient-specific testing with the goal to include underrepresented patient groups such as women and children early in the design process. As a part of this work, we have built Scandinavia's first hybrid mock circulatory loop (Interview), which enables testing of physical prototypes in interaction with a virtual model of the patient.

CardioLoop: A versatile hybrid mock circulatory loop

Development of test rigs that allow for patient-specific testing in combination with magnetic resonance imaging of 4-dimensional flow fields. This allows us to simulate not only average conditions but also tune to patient-specific parameters and simulate sleep or exercise, becoming a realistic alternative to animal trials.

Contact: Seraina Dual
Funded by: Vetenskapsrådet, Swedish Research Foundation
In collaboration with: Karolinska Institute (Asst. Prof. David Marlevi)

Aortic coarctation biomechanics during exercise

Predicting blood pressure gradient (∆P) across aortic coarctations during exercise through in vitro measurements in compliant aortic phantoms incorporated in a hybrid mock circulatory loop tuned to patient-specific medical record data.

Contact: Seraina Dual

Total Artificial Heart

Pre-clinical evaluation of the physiological control of a total artificial heart

The focus of this research is to advance the knowledge of total artificial hearts as a viable therapeutic option for patients suffering from heart failure... Our ultimate aim is to improve the safety and performance of these critical medical technologies.

Computational Analysis

Patient-specific computational modeling

As cardiovascular diseases continue to be the leading cause of mortality worldwide, computational and hybrid testing of treatment strategies such as heart pumps present a promising avenue for saving lives... This research integrates data-driven and model-based methods for clinic insight estimation and prediction. Lumped parameter model (LPM) for cardiovascular is applied in this research... The goal is to enhance the accuracy and performance of cardiovascular models, ultimately facilitating the creation of patient-specific digital twins.

Contact: Fangnan Xu
Funding: Smarter Electronics Systems, VINNOVA
Signal Processing

Physiology-guided signal processing

Digital Health Tools for non-invasive diagnosis of lung disease

Pulmonary hypertension (PH) is a strong prognostic factor in many cardiovascular diseases. We develop engineering tools to monitor, detect and predict PH using algorithms which integrate standard echocardiographic images. Our framework utilizes artificial intelligence to automate assessment of tricuspid regurgitation signals and detect image quality.

Contact: Seraina Dual
In collaboration with: Stanford University, Karolinska Institute

Sensors & Wearables

Healthcare monitoring: implantable & wearable

Self-powered biodegradable sensors

Development of Triboelectric nanogenerators (TENGs) that convert mechanical energy into electrical energy. These biocompatible and biodegradable sensors are designed for continuous health monitoring (heart rate, respiration, wound status) without the need for external power sources or batteries.

Device Innovation

Cardiac support technologies

Aortic Assist

Design and evaluation of extra-aortic counterpulsation devices. These soft robotic systems provide blood flow support while minimizing blood contact, reducing the risk of thrombosis and hemolysis.

Contact: Kyle Mudge, Seraina Dual

Soft robotic support for the right heart

Development of electrically-driven soft robotic devices for supporting blood flow to the lungs. This research addresses the clinical need for right heart support in heart failure patients.

Contact: Seraina Dual
