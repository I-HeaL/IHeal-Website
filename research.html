Research Areas

Addressing clinical needs through intelligent technological solutions. Our work spans robotics, simulation, and sensor integration to improve cardiovascular health outcomes.

Sensing & Actuation

Interactive Wearable Technologies

Developing smart, unobtrusive wearable devices for long-term cardiovascular monitoring and active rehabilitation. We focus on integrating soft sensors into textiles and actuating systems that provide physical assistance without restricting natural movement.

Experimental

Test-rigs for Patient-Specific Insight

Home to Scandinavia's first hybrid mock circulatory loop. We combine physical fluid dynamics with real-time computational models to simulate patient-specific hemodynamic conditions. This unique setup allows for the precise evaluation of cardiac assist devices under realistic physiological loads.

Read More
Total Artificial Heart

Pre-clinical Evaluation

Investigating the biomechanical interactions between Total Artificial Heart (TAH) systems and the anatomical environment. We aim to optimize control algorithms to reduce adverse events such as hemolysis and thrombosis.

Computational Analysis

Patient-Specific Modeling

Developing advanced Lumped Parameter Models (LPM) and Digital Twins to simulate complex cardiovascular pathologies. These models facilitate non-invasive diagnostics and allow clinicians to predict the outcome of surgical interventions before entering the operating room.
